Tow coupling & ground exceptions

Who pairs trailers to vehicles, when, and what happens when reality doesn't match the plan (asset under maintenance, damaged, or missing). The rule: the coordinator plans, the driver confirms actual, and asset status changes stay with admin — drivers report, they don't overwrite.

What "Confirm hitched" does

Creates a coupling record: trailer SH-45678 ↔ MAN TGX, hitched at 06:04, by driver Omar, at the yard's GPS. On the map the trailer now moves with the truck; mileage is attributed to the truck. Drop it at the site → "Unhitch" closes the coupling.

🚚 MAN TGX ⇄ hitched 06:04 ⇄ 🚐 SH 45678
"Hitched a different trailer"

Driver picks the trailer he actually coupled from a list of units on this hire. If it's not on the hire, it becomes a substitution request → coordinator approves (next section). The plan is preserved for the record; the actual is logged.

3 · Exception — planned trailer is under maintenance / unfit

1Driver flags it
"SH-45678 has a flat / is tagged for service." Driver taps Report problem. He cannot mark it available — only report.
→
2System reacts instantly
Auto-creates a maintenance flag/incident on SH-45678 so it can't be re-assigned. Surfaces available equivalents — same category/spec, not on hire, not in maintenance.
→
3Driver proposes substitute
Picks "40ft Catering Trailer SH-12345" as a replacement and confirms hitched — status goes pending approval.
→
4Coordinator approves
Sees the swap on the command center, approves (or calls the driver). Contract/billing updates to the substitute; the hire continues. resolved

Two guardrails so it rarely gets this far: ① Planning gate — when the coordinator assigns an asset that's In Maintenance or double-booked for those dates, the booking warns/blocks immediately. ② Dispatch gate — the hire can't move to "Dispatched" while any assigned unit isn't Available (same pattern as the driver-compliance gate already built).

4 · Edge cases & the rule for each

SituationBest practice
Driver couples a trailer not on the hireAllowed but flagged as substitution → coordinator approves; keeps contract honest.
Asset found damaged at pickupDriver runs the check-out inspection (photos) → if damage > baseline, auto-incident + can't dispatch until reviewed.
No equivalent available for a substituteSystem says so; coordinator decides: delay, source externally, or partial dispatch. Never a silent gap.
Driver offline at the yard (no signal)Portal queues the confirm/coupling locally and syncs when back online (same offline pattern the driver portal already uses).
One truck makes multiple trips (drops trailer A, returns for B)Each coupling is its own timestamped event; the truck's mileage spans all legs.
Trailer moved by a third-party / client truckCoupling can record "external tow" (no internal vehicle) so the map still tracks the trailer.
